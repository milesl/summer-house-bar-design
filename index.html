OSB Summer House Bar

A standing-height bar with sculptural 3D wave front — vertical OSB slices at staggered depths with warm LED uplighting. Open-back design with three internal shelves and a centre divider. Optimised to 1220mm width — everything from just two sheets.

Design highlights: No rear panel — open back with three internal shelves for bottles, glasses, and mixers. Centre divider prevents shelf sag and organises storage. No wall-mounted shelving needed. Wave panels cut from 18mm offcuts for a chunkier feel. Everything from 2 standard sheets at ~95% utilisation.

04 — Build Instructions

Step-by-Step

1

Frame Construction

01

Cut All CLS Timber

Cut all 13 pieces from your 5× 2.4m CLS lengths before assembly. Mark each piece with its role as you cut.

Length Cuts Waste
1 1180 + 1180 40mm
2 1180 + 1180 40mm
3 950 + 950 + 480 20mm
4 950 + 950 + 480 20mm
5 950 + 480 + 480 490mm

You should have: 4× long rails (1180mm), 4× short rails (480mm), 5× posts (950mm). Label them LR, SR, P.

Cut all timber first. Label every piece. The 490mm offcut from length 5 is handy for spacer blocks later.

02

Build Top & Bottom Frames

Build two identical rectangles — each from 2× long rails (1180mm) and 2× short rails (480mm).

1. Lay two long rails parallel, 480mm apart (outside to outside).
2. Butt short rails between the long rails at each end.
3. Pre-drill and screw with 2× 4.0×50mm screws per corner (8 per frame).
4. Check square — measure both diagonals. They should match within 2mm. Adjust before screws are fully tight.

03

Assemble Posts & Frames

1. Stand one frame on the floor — this is the bottom frame.
2. Screw the 4 corner posts (950mm) to the inside corners of the bottom frame. Pre-drill, then 2× 4.0×50mm screws per post from outside the frame into the end grain.
3. Screw the mid front post to the centre of the front long rail (~590mm from each end).
4. Lift the top frame onto the posts. Align flush and screw down — same method, 2 screws per post.
5. Check plumb on all posts with a spirit level. Adjust before tightening fully.

04

Fix Frame to Wall & Floor

Position the assembled frame against the wall where the bar will live.

Wall fixings: Drill through the rear top and bottom rails into the wall. Use 6× 100mm coach screws — 3 along the top rail, 3 along the bottom. For masonry: drill 10mm holes, insert wall plugs first.

Floor fixings: Drill down through the bottom frame into the floor. Use 4× M10×75mm anchors — one near each corner.

Timber floor? Skip the anchors — use 100mm screws straight down through the bottom rail instead. Stud wall? Skip wall plugs — screw directly into studs.

2

Side Cladding

05

Cut Side Panels

From the 11mm OSB sheet, cut 2× side panels at 925 × 550mm. These are 25mm shorter than the 950mm carcass height — the gap at the top is hidden under the bar top overhang.

Refer to the Sheet 2 cut layout — side panels come from the left portion of the sheet. If the merchant crosscut the sheet at 925mm, you just need to rip at 550mm.

06

Glue & Pin Side Panels

1. Apply PVA glue to the outer faces of the corner posts and side rails.
2. Align the panel flush with the top of the frame and flush with the front posts.
3. Pin with 30mm panel pins at ~150mm centres around the edges.
4. Repeat for the other side.

The bottom edge of the panel will sit ~25mm above the floor. This gap is invisible from the front and helps if the floor is uneven.

3

Internal Shelving

07

Cut & Fit Shelf Cleats

Cut small cleats (~100 × 45mm) from 11mm offcuts. You need roughly 12 cleats — 2 per shelf per side (left and right of the divider), attached to the side panels and corner posts.

Screw cleats to the inner faces at these heights from the top of the bottom frame:

Bottom shelf (mixers): 150mm
Middle shelf (glasses): 450mm
Top shelf (spirits): 700mm

Use 3.5×25mm screws, 2 per cleat. Pre-drill the 11mm OSB to avoid splitting.

Tip: Cut a spacer block to each height and use it to position all cleats at that level consistently.

08

Cut & Fit Centre Divider

Cut the centre divider at 875 × 480mm from 11mm OSB. This sits vertically in the middle of the carcass, running front to back.

1. Mark the centre of the front and rear bottom rails (~590mm from each end).
2. Stand the divider vertically between the bottom shelf cleats and the underside of the top frame.
3. Screw through the front and rear bottom rails into the divider edge.
4. Screw down through the top frame rail into the top edge.

09

Cut & Fit Shelves

Cut three shelves from 11mm OSB:

Bottom: 1080 × 250mm
Middle: 1080 × 200mm
Top: 1080 × 110mm

Each shelf spans the full carcass width (between the posts). Notch the centre of each shelf (~6mm wide) to fit around the divider, or cut each shelf as two halves.

Slide shelves onto their cleats. Pin or screw down with 3.5×25mm screws into the cleats to prevent movement.

Easiest approach: Cut shelves full width and notch for the divider — one straight jigsaw cut per shelf. More rigid than two separate halves.

4

Wave Front & LEDs

10

Mark & Cut Wave Panels

From the 18mm OSB offcuts (Sheet 1 remainder), cut 9–10 panels at 700 × 100mm.

1. Mark straight 100mm-wide strips across the offcut piece.
2. Cut the strips with a circular saw or jigsaw with a fence.
3. On the front face of each panel, draw a wavy line using a flexible batten or freehand. Vary the wave — some gentle curves, some more pronounced. Maximum depth variation ~140mm.

11

Shape & Sand Panels

1. Clamp each panel and jigsaw along the wavy line.
2. Rough-sand the wavy edge with an 80-grit flap disc on an angle grinder — this shapes quickly and gives a sculptural texture.
3. Finish-sand with 120-grit by hand. Round over any sharp edges.

12

Install LED Strip

Fix the LED strip (2700K, IP65) along the front face of the bottom front rail, LEDs facing upward.

1. Peel off the adhesive backing and press along the rail.
2. Add cable clips every 200mm for security.
3. Route the cable out through the open back — no drilling needed.
4. Connect the driver and test.

Test the LEDs now. Once wave panels are fixed, accessing the strip is much harder. Check for dead spots and ensure even brightness.

13

Fix Wave Panels

Cut spacer blocks at varying depths (20–50mm) from offcuts. These create the wave effect when viewed from the side.

1. Cut a 25mm timber strip as a gap spacer jig.
2. Start at the left end. Place the first spacer block on the bottom rail, then the first panel against it. Screw through the panel base into the bottom rail.
3. Place the 25mm spacer jig, then the next panel with a different depth spacer.
4. Continue left to right, alternating spacer depths: e.g. 30, 50, 20, 45, 25, 50, 30, 40, 20mm.
5. Screw each panel top into the front top rail as well.

5

Bar Top

14

Laminate Bar Top

Glue the underlay (1180 × 510mm, 11mm) to the underside of the bar top (1220 × 550mm, 18mm).

1. Apply PVA glue evenly across the underlay.
2. Centre it on the underside of the bar top: ~20mm inset from each side, ~20mm from the rear edge.
3. Clamp or weight down evenly. Leave for at least 1 hour.

15

Router Edges & Fix to Frame

1. Router a 6mm roundover on all exposed top edges of the bar top. Sand smooth with 120-grit.
2. Place the laminated bar top on the frame:
— 200mm front overhang (past the front posts)
— ~20mm side overhangs
— Flush at the rear
3. From underneath, screw up through the top frame rails into the underlay with 3.5×25mm screws — 6–8 screws evenly spaced. The screws bite into the 11mm underlay without penetrating the 18mm bar top surface.

6

Finishing

16

Final Sand

Sand the entire bar with 120-grit. Pay extra attention to the bar top surface, wave panel faces, and any visible edges. Remove all dust with a tack cloth or damp rag before varnishing.

17

Varnish

1. Apply 2–3 coats clear satin polyurethane. Bar top gets 3 coats minimum — it takes the most wear.
2. Sand lightly with 240-grit between coats. Wipe off dust.
3. Allow 4–6 hours drying between coats.

Leave shelves bare. Raw OSB grips better — bottles and glasses won't slide around. A light wax is fine if you want some protection.

18

Connect LEDs & Stock

1. Connect the LED driver. Route the cable neatly behind the bar — use cable clips on the rear posts.
2. Test the LEDs one final time.
3. Stock the shelves: spirits and glasses on one side of the divider, mixers and tools on the other.

You're done. Enjoy the bar.

07 — Tips

Workshop Notes

OSB3 only. Green edge. OSB2 swells in summer houses.

Ask the merchant to crosscut. Most do the first few cuts free. Get 18mm crosscut at 550mm, 11mm at 925mm. Do fine cuts at home.

18mm wave panels are chunkier than the original 9mm spec. Better shadow-casting, more substantial feel, and no extra sheet needed.

Centre divider (875mm tall) prevents shelf sag at 1080mm span and organises storage. Splits the interior into 6 cubbyholes.

Open back means no rear panel to cut and fit, and easy access to all three shelves. The wall behind the bar becomes the visual backdrop — consider painting it a dark colour to contrast with the OSB.

LED: IP65, 2700K. Fix to bottom rail BEFORE wave panels. Test first.

Side panels at 925mm (25mm shorter than carcass). Gap hidden under bar top.

Further savings: Reclaimed OSB from skips/Marketplace. OSB2 for internal shelves (dry, protected). Danish oil instead of polyurethane.

08 — Bonus Build

Wall-Mounted Box Shelves

Three matching box shelves from the bar's offcuts — no extra sheet material needed. Mount above or beside the bar for bottles, glasses, or display. Same OSB aesthetic as the bar.

Box Shelf Dimensions

External size 380 × 250 × 200mm (w × h × d)
Internal size 344 × 214 × 200mm
Quantity 3 shelves
Top & bottom 380 × 200mm (18mm)
Sides 214 × 200mm (18mm)
Back panel 344 × 214mm (11mm)
Wall fixings 4× screws per shelf through back

Each shelf holds 3–4 spirit bottles standing, a row of wine bottles on their side, or a mix of glasses and short bottles.

Material Source

18mm pieces From Sheet 1 spare (890 × 1220)
11mm back panels From Sheet 2 spare (445 × 1080)
Extra fixings 12× screws + 12× wall plugs

No extra materials to buy — everything comes from the bar's offcuts and your existing screws, glue, and varnish.

Offcut Cut Plans

Box Shelf Assembly

1

Build Each Box

01

Cut All Pieces

From the 18mm offcut, rip 3 strips at 200mm wide. Then crosscut each strip into:

2× at 380mm (top and bottom) + 2× at 214mm (sides).

From the 11mm offcut, cut 3× back panels at 344 × 214mm.

Total for 3 shelves: 6× tops/bottoms, 6× sides, 3× backs.

02

Assemble Boxes

For each box:

1. Stand the two sides upright, 344mm apart (inside to inside).
2. Place the bottom piece on top of the sides, flush at the back. Pre-drill and screw down with 2× 4.0×50mm screws per side (4 total).
3. Flip and attach the top piece — same method.
4. Slide the 11mm back panel into the recess at the back. It should sit flush with the rear edges. Pin with 30mm panel pins around the edges.
5. Check square — the back panel will hold everything true.

Apply PVA glue to all joints before screwing — it makes a huge difference to rigidity on 18mm OSB. Wipe off squeeze-out with a damp cloth.

03

Finish & Mount

1. Sand all faces with 120-grit. Round over front edges by hand.
2. Apply 2 coats clear satin polyurethane. Sand 240-grit between coats.
3. To mount: hold the shelf in position on the wall. Drill 4× holes through the back panel into the wall (one in each corner area). Fix with 4× 50mm screws into wall plugs or studs.

Arrangement ideas: Stagger the 3 boxes at different heights beside or above the bar. Or stack them in an asymmetric grid. Leave 50–80mm gaps between boxes for visual breathing room.
