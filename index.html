OSB Summer House Bar

A standing-height bar with sculptural 3D wave front — vertical OSB slices at staggered depths with warm LED uplighting. Open-back design with three internal shelves and a centre divider. Optimised to 1220mm width — everything from just two sheets.

04 — Build Instructions

Step-by-Step

1

Frame Construction

01

Build & Fix Frame

Build two identical rectangles from CLS: 1180mm long rails + 480mm short rails. Check square. Cut 4 corner posts + 1 mid front post at 950mm. Assemble, check plumb. Fix to wall with 6× coach screws, floor with 4× anchors.

2

Side Cladding

02

Fit Side Panels

Glue and pin 2× side panels (925 × 550mm, 11mm) to the side faces of the frame. 25mm shorter than carcass height — hidden under bar top. No rear panel — the back stays open for shelf access.

3

Internal Shelving

03

Fit Shelf Cleats

Cut small cleats (~100 × 45mm) from 11mm offcuts. Screw cleats to the inner faces of the side panels and corner posts at shelf heights. Position shelves at approximately:

Bottom shelf (mixers): 150mm from carcass floor. Middle shelf (glasses): 450mm from floor. Top shelf (spirits): 700mm from floor (just below bar top).

Adjust heights to suit your bottles and glassware.

04

Fit Centre Divider & Shelves

Cut the centre divider at 875 × 480mm from 11mm OSB. This sits vertically in the middle of the carcass, running front to back, between the bottom shelf and the underside of the bar top. Screw to front and rear bottom rails and to the underside of the top frame.

Slide the three shelves into place on their cleats, one each side of the divider. Pin or screw down into the cleats. The divider prevents sag and splits the storage into two organised sections — spirits and glasses on one side, mixers and tools on the other.

4

Wave Front & LEDs

05

Cut & Sand Wave Panels

Cut 9–10 panels at 700 × 100mm from 18mm offcuts. Wave-cut the front face with a jigsaw. Vary amplitude and frequency per panel. Sand with 80-grit flap disc then 120-grit by hand.

06

LED Strip & Wave Panels

Before panels: Fix LED strip (2700K, IP65) along the front bottom rail, facing up. Test.

Prepare spacer blocks at varying depths. Fix panels left to right with 25mm gaps, alternating depths for the ripple effect. Use a timber strip as a gap spacer jig.

5

Bar Top

07

Laminate & Fix

Glue underlay (1180 × 510, 11mm) to underside of bar top (1220 × 550, 18mm). Router roundover on edges. Fix to frame: 200mm front overhang, 20mm sides, flush rear.

6

Finishing

08

Sand, Varnish, Done

Sand everything 120-grit. 2–3 coats clear satin polyurethane (bar top gets 3). Sand 240-grit between coats. Connect LEDs. Stock the shelves.

07 — Tips

Workshop Notes

OSB3 only. Green edge. OSB2 swells in summer houses.

Ask the merchant to crosscut. Most do the first few cuts free. Get 18mm crosscut at 550mm, 11mm at 925mm. Do fine cuts at home.

18mm wave panels are chunkier than the original 9mm spec. Better shadow-casting, more substantial feel, and no extra sheet needed.

Centre divider (875mm tall) prevents shelf sag at 1080mm span and organises storage. Splits the interior into 6 cubbyholes.

Open back means no rear panel to cut and fit, and easy access to all three shelves. The wall behind the bar becomes the visual backdrop — consider painting it a dark colour to contrast with the OSB.

LED: IP65, 2700K. Fix to bottom rail BEFORE wave panels. Test first.

Side panels at 925mm (25mm shorter than carcass). Gap hidden under bar top.

Further savings: Reclaimed OSB from skips/Marketplace. OSB2 for internal shelves (dry, protected). Danish oil instead of polyurethane.
